CrispCut Computer Vision Playground

Below are several examples of several common computer vision 'filters' or algorithms:

- Canny edge detection is used to find the edges and images and is used for segmenting things and finding corners of surfaces. Notice how clearly it makes features like doorways and table edges stand out.

- Adaptive Gaussian Thresholding is used to evenly blend out lighting in an image. Its a bit hard to tell because you are seeing it applied to a grey version of the origional image, but notice how it highlights more shadowy parts of the image.

- Background Subtraction is used to detect motion in a video. Frames of the video are -more or less- subtracted from eachother in pairs of two or sometimes more (to detect longer trends in motion and improve accurace). All that remains is the differences between the two frames ie. wherever there was motion.
